  DOCUMENT 3: UNAUTHORISED ACTION PENALTIES & ENFORCEMENT POLICY  

This document governs all consequences for actions taken without the required approval of the Supreme Authority or delegated Core Committee.



1. Definition of Unauthorised Action

An "Unauthorised Action" is any act, omission, communication, commitment, representation, or decision taken by any TDC participant that:

Falls within the Mandatory Approval categories in Document 2, Section 3, AND

Was taken without prior written approval from the President/CEO/Director or a formally delegated Core Committee officer



An Unauthorised Action is also any act taken by a participant that:

Exceeds the scope of the authority explicitly granted to that participant's role tier

Purports to bind TDC to any obligation without proper authorisation

Deliberately misrepresents TDC's position, products, or intentions to any third party

Attempts to circumvent, undermine, or override decisions of the Supreme Authority

2. Classification of Unauthorised Actions

CLASS

DESCRIPTION

BASE PENALTY

Class I — Minor

Taking a routine action slightly outside defined scope, with no material harm to TDC or third parties. Examples: communicating project updates to members without approval, minor formatting changes to official docs.

Written warning. Formal record entry. Mandatory re-training.

Class II — Moderate

Actions with potential reputational, financial, or legal consequences for TDC. Examples: making promises to participants about distributions, posting on TDC social media without approval, recruiting new members without authorisation.

Formal written warning + 30-day privilege suspension + forfeiture of any pending bonus or additional distribution.

Class III — Serious

Actions that expose TDC to legal liability, financial harm, or reputational damage. Examples: signing contracts on TDC's behalf, launching or taking down products, disclosing confidential financials.

Immediate suspension pending investigation + potential permanent removal + forfeiture of all financial entitlements + potential legal action.

Class IV — Critical

Actions that constitute fraud, sabotage, IP theft, or direct attack on TDC or the Supreme Authority. Examples: forking TDC code for personal use, initiating false legal claims, recruiting TDC members for a competing platform, publicly defaming TDC leadership.

Immediate permanent removal + full legal prosecution + demand for damages + injunctive relief sought + civil and criminal referral as appropriate.



3. Penalty Escalation

Penalties escalate automatically on repeat violations:

First violation of any class: base penalty as above

Second violation within 12 months: base penalty + one class upgrade (e.g. Class I treated as Class II)

Third violation of any class: immediate permanent removal from TDC regardless of class

Any single Class IV violation: immediate permanent removal with no appeal

4. Financial Penalties

In addition to the status-based penalties above, participants who cause measurable financial harm to TDC through Unauthorised Actions are personally liable for:

All direct costs incurred by TDC in remedying the Unauthorised Action

All legal fees, arbitration costs, and professional fees incurred by TDC in connection with the Unauthorised Action

All consequential losses reasonably attributable to the Unauthorised Action, including but not limited to lost partnerships, cancelled contracts, or damaged relationships

Any fines or regulatory penalties imposed on TDC arising from the Unauthorised Action



⚠  PERSONAL LIABILITY FOR UNAUTHORISED FINANCIAL COMMITMENTS

Any participant who commits TDC to a financial obligation without authorisation is personally liable for that entire obligation. TDC will not honour unauthorised financial commitments and will seek full recovery from the individual responsible.

5. Enforcement Process

5.1 Investigation

Upon receipt of a report or identification of a potential Unauthorised Action, the Core Committee will investigate. The investigated participant may provide a written explanation within 5 business days. The Supreme Authority makes the final determination.

5.2 Hearing

For Class III and Class IV violations, the participant is entitled to present their case in writing before a final determination. Oral hearings are at the Supreme Authority's discretion. The Supreme Authority's decision is final.

5.3 Enforcement

Penalties are enforced immediately upon determination. Financial penalties are pursued through arbitration or civil courts as appropriate. TDC reserves the right to seek emergency injunctive relief without notice for Class IV violations.

5.4 No Internal Appeal

The Supreme Authority's penalty decision is final and non-appealable within TDC. Participants retain the right to challenge decisions through the external arbitration process set out in Document 1, Section 10.

6. Reporting Obligations

Any participant who becomes aware of an Unauthorised Action by another participant is obligated to report it to the Core Committee within 48 hours. Failure to report a known Unauthorised Action may constitute a Class II violation. Reports made in good faith are protected from retaliation.
